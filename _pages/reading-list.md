Running list of texts to read, with source and date recorded.

## To read

- Vannevar Bush, "As We May Think" (1945). Source: Mike Caulfield, ["The Garden and the Stream: A Technopastoral"](https://hapgood.us/2015/10/17/the-garden-and-the-stream-a-technopastoral/) (2015). Caulfield assumes his audience has read this essay and calls it essential: *"I'm going to assume most people in the room here have read Vannevar Bush's 1945 essay As We May Think. If you haven't read it yet, you need to."* Recorded 26 June 2026.
- Dave Cormier, ["Community Learning — Every 'We' Makes a 'Them'"](https://davecormier.com/edblog/2015/08/27/community-learning-every-we-makes-a-them/) (2015). Source: Caulfield references Cormier's post: *"Dave Cormier had an interesting post a month or so ago about how 'every we makes a them.'"* Recorded 26 June 2026.
- Martin Amis’s essays. Source: Zadie Smith: *“I read Carver. Julio Cortázar. Amis’s essays. Baldwin. Lorrie Moore. Capote. Saramago. Larkin. Wodehouse. Anything, anything at all, that doesn’t sound like me.”* Recorded 26 June 2026.
- David Foster Wallace, “Forever Overhead.” Source: Zadie Smith: *“If I could choose one story to be printed alongside this article as demonstration, it would be Foster Wallace’s ‘Forever Overhead’, a 10-page effort that has come to obsess me quite as much as the miserable death of poor, terminally middle-class Ivan.”* Recorded 26 June 2026.

## Read

Pieces worth remembering.

- Joseph Heath, ["Harari vs. Henrich,"](https://josephheath.substack.com/p/harari-vs-henrich) *Substack* (2026). Heath critiques Harari's evolutionary theory in *Sapiens* as scientifically flawed, arguing it runs directly counter to Henrich's more rigorous framework for how humans developed intelligence, language, cooperation, and culture. Note: Could echolalia have emerged through the same adaptive pathway? Recorded 26 June 2026.
- Robert Minto, ["Rank and File,"](https://reallifemag.com/rank-and-file/) *Real Life* (2021). Minto examines his decade-long search for the perfect note-taking system, arguing that tools like Roam Research and the zettelkasten method ultimately fail to substitute for genuine intellectual work and critical thinking. Recorded 26 June 2026.
