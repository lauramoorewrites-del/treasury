- Ana Vieyra, ["On a recent controversy regarding clarity in philosophical writing,"](https://epochemagazine.org/88/on-a-recent-controversy-regarding-clarity-in-philosophical-writing/) *Epoché Magazine* (2026). Accuses Muñoz of "logocide" — killing philosophy by demanding clarity — and defends obscurity as a feature of semantically rich thought.
- Daniel Muñoz, ["Is Daniel Muñoz an agent of the death of philosophy itself?"](https://bigifftrue.substack.com/p/is-daniel-munoz-an-agent-of-the-death) *Big iff True* (2026). Muñoz's response. He distinguishes between obscurity caused by difficult *ideas* and obscurity caused by poor *expression*, and argues that truly understanding something means being able to explain it at multiple levels.
