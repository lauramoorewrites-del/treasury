Vodolazkin (b. 1964) is a Doctor of Philological Sciences and senior researcher in Old Russian Literature at the Pushkin House (Institute of Russian Literature, Russian Academy of Sciences), where he studied under Dmitry Likhachev. He has been there since 1990.

## Novels

- *Похищение Европы* (The Abduction of Europa), 2005 — not translated
- *Соловьёв и Ларионов*, 2009 — English: [*Solovyov and Larionov*](https://bookshop.org/search?q=9781786073440), tr. Lisa C. Hayden (Oneworld, 2018)
- *Лавр*, 2012 — English: [*Laurus*](https://bookshop.org/search?q=9781786070043), tr. Lisa C. Hayden (Oneworld, 2015). Won the Big Book Prize and Yasnaya Polyana Prize (2013); Hayden's translation won the Read Russia Prize (2016). Translated into 35+ languages.
- *Авиатор*, 2016 — English: [*The Aviator*](https://bookshop.org/search?q=9781786072153), tr. Lisa C. Hayden (Oneworld, 2018)
- *Брисбен*, 2018/2019 — English: [*Brisbane*](https://bookshop.org/search?q=9781636080642), tr. Marian Schwartz (Plough, 2022)
- *Оправдание Острова*, 2020 — English: [*A History of the Island*](https://bookshop.org/search?q=9781636081113), tr. Lisa C. Hayden (Plough, 2023). Longlisted for the 2024 International Dublin Literary Award.
- *Чагин*, 2022 — not translated
- *Последнее дело майора Чистова* (The Last Case of Major Chistov), May 2026 — not translated (just published). A detective novel about the murder of an AI scholar; Vodolazkin has described it as a genre experiment.

## Essay and story collections

- *Инструмент языка: О людях и словах* (The Instrument of Language: On People and Words), essays, 2012 — not translated
- *Совсем другое время* (A Completely Different Time), 2013 — contains *Solovyov and Larionov*, the novella *Blizkie druz'ia* (Close Friends), and several stories. Not translated as a collection.
- *Дом и остров, или Инструмент языка* (Home and Island, or The Instrument of Language), expanded reissue of the essay collection, 2014 — not translated
- *Стоп-кадр. Ностальгия* (Freeze Frame. Nostalgia), stories and essays, 2015 — not translated
- *Идти бестрепетно: Между литературой и жизнью* (Walk Without Trepidation: Between Literature and Life), autobiographical essays, 2020 — covers his childhood in Kyiv, work at Pushkin House, the transition from medievalist to novelist, and backstories of his major novels. Not translated.

## Drama

- *Пара пьес* (A Pair of Plays), 2014 — contains *Muzei* (Museum, about the Kirov assassination) and *Parodist* (comedy-detective-family drama)
- *Петербургские драмы* (Petersburg Dramas), 2015 — reissue of the same two plays
- *Сестра четырёх* (Sister of Four), 2020 — four plays: the title play (absurdist hospital drama), *Parodist*, *Muzei*, and *Mikropol'* (a satire blending modern setting with ancient Greek chorus)

None of the plays have been translated into English.

## Scholarly and academic works

Dissertations:

- Kandidat (PhD), 1990: "The Chronicle of George Amartolus in Old Russian Literature" (under Likhachev at Pushkin House)
- Doctoral, 2000: "World History in the Literature of Ancient Rus' (Based on Chronographic and Palaea Narrative of the 11th–15th Centuries)"

Monographs:

- *Кирилл, Ферапонт и Мартиниан Белозерские* (co-authored with G.M. Prokhorov et al.), 1993 — hagiographic study of three medieval Russian saints
- *Всемирная история в литературе Древней Руси* (World History in the Literature of Ancient Rus'), Munich 2000; 2nd ed. St. Petersburg: Dmitry Bulanin, 2008 — his scholarly magnum opus on how medieval Russian writers understood universal history through chronographic and Palaea traditions, with particular attention to non-linear time perception and typological thinking
- *Дмитрий Лихачёв и его эпоха* (Dmitry Likhachev and His Era), compiled/edited by Vodolazkin, 2002 (2nd ed. 2006) — biographical-documentary volume about his mentor
- *Часть суши, окружённая небом* (A Portion of Land Surrounded by Sky: Solovetsk Texts and Images), compiled/edited by Vodolazkin et al., 2011 — anthology of texts and images on the Solovetsky Islands and monastery

Editorial work:

- *Текст и традиция* (Text and Tradition), scholarly almanac, vols. 1–7 (2013–2019), editor-in-chief. Published jointly by Pushkin House and the Yasnaya Polyana Museum-Estate.

He is credited with over 100 individual scholarly articles on Old Russian historical narrative, biblical exegesis in medieval Slavic literature, chronographic traditions, the Palaea, and hagiography. A full article-level listing is maintained at Pushkin House but isn't available in a single public-facing source.

None of the scholarly works have been translated into English.
