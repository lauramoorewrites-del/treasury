- Samuel Taylor Coleridge and *Kubla Khan* (1816). *"All the images rose up before him as things, with a parallel production of the correspondent expressions, without any sensation or consciousness of effort."*
- Harriet Beecher Stowe and *Uncle Tom's Cabin* (1852). *"The story can less be said to have been composed by her than imposed upon her."*
- Mozart and his musical compositions. Entire compositions arrived simultaneously; he simply transcribed what was already in his mind.
- Srinivasa Ramanujan and his mathematical equations and formulas. Many arrived through dreams, reportedly sent by the Hindu goddess Namagiri.
- Friedrich August Kekulé and the benzene ring structure (1890). The solution came from a dream featuring a snake consuming its own tail, revealing the circular atomic arrangement.
- Richard Feynman and his Nobel Prize-winning insight. The discovery emerged while observing a spinning plate in a cafeteria.
