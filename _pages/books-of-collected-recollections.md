Books that assemble a portrait of someone through the memories and testimonies of people who knew them.

## Literary figures

- Joan Russell Noble (ed.), *Recollections of Virginia Woolf By Her Contemporaries* (1972). Contributions from Duncan Grant, Rebecca West, T.S. Eliot, E.M. Forster, and a member of Woolf's household staff.
- Maxim Gorky, *Reminiscences of Tolstoy, Chekhov and Andreev* (1920/1934). Originally published by the Hogarth Press. Fragmentary notes from living near Tolstoy during his illness in Crimea; the Chekhov section appeared in Russia in 1906. Leonard Woolf called the Tolstoy portrait "one of the most remarkable biographical pieces ever written."
- Audrey Coppard and Bernard Crick (eds.), *Orwell Remembered* (1984). Personal reminiscences from people who knew Orwell, spanning childhood to his death at 46.
- David N. Thomas (ed.), *Dylan Remembered*, Vols. 1 & 2 (2003/2004). Drawn from archive tapes of interviews conducted by journalist Colin Edwards. Interviewees include Fred Janes, Mably Owen, Vernon Watkins, Glyn Jones, and villagers from Thomas's haunts.
- Anatoly Nayman, *Remembering Anna Akhmatova* (1991). Nayman was Akhmatova's literary secretary and fellow poet. Uses her letters, poems, and direct speech alongside personal recollection. Introduction by Joseph Brodsky.

## Philosophy

- Rush Rhees (ed.), *Recollections of Wittgenstein* (1984). Contributions from Wittgenstein's sister Hermine, Fania Pascal, F.R. Leavis, John King, and M. O'C. Drury.

## Music

- Malcolm Gillies, *Bartók Remembered*. Collected recollections of the composer from those who knew him.

## Visual arts and cultural figures

- Jean Stein and George Plimpton, *Edie: An American Biography* (1982). Oral biography of Edie Sedgwick assembled from interviews with over 200 people, including Truman Capote, Patti Smith, Andy Warhol, Gore Vidal, and Diana Vreeland. 
- Patricia Treece, *A Man for Others: Maximilian Kolbe*. Life of the Franciscan saint told entirely through first-person testimonies from family, fellow friars, and death camp survivors.

## Adjacent

- Jean Cocteau, *My Contemporaries*. Cocteau's intimate portraits of Proust, Piaf, Colette, and other figures from Bohemian Paris. Single-author rather than multi-voice.
