A personal theory: some people who claim not to like kids may have a sound sensitivity. Kids are very loud. This theory arises from Jameela Jamil’s controversial Substack piece where she lists the reasons she isn’t having kids, and among other things, called them “annoying” and “noisy.” (This is from memory — the piece is now paywalled). Growing up, I had a friend that my dad hated to have over because, he said, she was too noisy.

- **Béla Bartók.** *“He always looked for a flat in quiet districts, for any noise coming through the walls upset him beyond endurance. He always chose the most isolated room for his study, and tried to make it sound-proof by fitting on a double-door by some means or other. For his part, he took great care not to disturb other people’s peace; when practising, he would shut his windows, even on hot days, in order not to disturb other people.”* Source unconfirmed; likely Serge Moreux, *Béla Bartók* (1953).
- **Arthur Schopenhauer.** Apparently measured a person's intelligence by their "sensitivity to noise." Via [Zadie Smith](/treasury/topics/zadie-smith/), ["The Instrumentalist,"](https://www.nybooks.com/articles/2023/01/19/the-instrumentalist-tar-todd-field-zadie-smith/) *The New York Review of Books* (2023): *"Schopenhauer apparently measured a person's intelligence by their 'sensitivity to noise'—or so a colleague informs Tár during one of her many meetings—and back at the loft she is woken in the night by strange noises she can't identify."*
- **Virginia Woolf.** Duncan Grant recalled: *“She was very sensitive to sound, and the noise from the mews and street was severe.”* From *Recollections of Virginia Woolf By Her Contemporaries* (1972), ed. Joan Russell Noble.
