- **Arthur Schopenhauer.** Apparently measured a person's intelligence by their "sensitivity to noise." Via Zadie Smith, ["The Instrumentalist,"](https://www.nybooks.com/articles/2023/01/19/the-instrumentalist-tar-todd-field-zadie-smith/) *The New York Review of Books* (2023): *"Schopenhauer apparently measured a person's intelligence by their 'sensitivity to noise'—or so a colleague informs Tár during one of her many meetings—and back at the loft she is woken in the night by strange noises she can't identify."*
- **Virginia Woolf.** Duncan Grant recalled: *"She was very sensitive to sound, and the noise from the mews and street was severe."* From *Recollections of Virginia Woolf By Her Contemporaries* (1972), ed. Joan Russell Noble.
